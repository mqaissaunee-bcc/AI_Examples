LLM Guide for STEM Questions

    
STEM question generation requires more careful LLM selection and verification than humanities or professional certification content. Here's what you need to know.

  


  

    
⚠ The Math Accuracy Problem

    
All current LLMs make arithmetic and algebraic errors. The frequency varies by model and problem complexity, but no LLM can be trusted to produce 100% numerically correct STEM questions without human verification. Common failure modes:

    
    
      
  • Arithmetic errors in multi-step calculations (especially with fractions, exponents, trig)
    • 
      
  • Inconsistent units — mixing SI and US, or dropping units mid-calculation
    • 
      
  • Incorrect distractor derivation — distractors that don't correspond to real student errors
    • 
      
  • LaTeX rendering errors — malformed expressions that don't render correctly
    • 
      
  • Self-contradiction — stem assumes one scenario, worked solution solves a different one
    •
