⚠ Theoretical vs. Observed Exposure — A Critical Distinction for Workshop Discussion Most headlines cite theoretical AI capability: 94% of Computer & Math tasks are technically LLM-feasible. Anthropic's 2026 Economic Index — built from real Claude usage in professional contexts — shows only 35.8% of those tasks are actually being performed by AI today. That 58-point gap reflects model limitations, legal/compliance constraints, integration costs, and adoption lag. Scores in this tool reflect observed deployment, not theoretical potential — a distinction that matters enormously for workforce planning.

Data Sources & Methodology — May 2026 Edition Exposure scores are drawn from Anthropic's Economic Index (Massenkoff & McCrory, March 2026) — the first measure built from real AI deployment data (actual Claude usage) rather than theoretical capability, and the only one that correlates with independent BLS employment projections. O*NET task data provides the occupational backbone.

Adaptability scores follow GovAI / Brookings methodology (Manning & Aguirre, 2026): education level, work experience diversity, worker age, savings, and local labor market strength.

Trend indicators are directional signals (not precise measurements) based on: Anthropic Index updates, Goldman Sachs Research (March 2026), BLS 2024–2034 projections, and PwC AI Jobs Barometer (2025). Adaptability trends reflect shifts in reskilling access, adjacent job growth, and demographic changes in each occupation's workforce. Dot size = approximate US employment (BLS 2025).
