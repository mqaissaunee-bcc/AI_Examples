⚠ Theoretical vs. Observed Exposure — A Critical Distinction Most headlines cite theoretical AI capability scores: 94% of Computer & Math tasks are technically LLM-feasible. But Anthropic's 2026 Economic Index, built from real Claude usage data, shows only 35.8% of those tasks are actually being performed by AI today. That 58-point gap reflects model limitations, legal/compliance constraints, integration costs, and adoption lag. The scores in this tool reflect observed deployment, not theoretical potential.

Data Sources & Methodology — May 2026 Edition Exposure scores are drawn from Anthropic's Economic Index (Massenkoff & McCrory, March 2026), which combines O*NET task data, Eloundou et al. theoretical feasibility scores, and actual Claude usage from professional contexts. This "Observed Exposure" measure is the first built from real AI deployment data rather than theoretical capability, and uniquely correlates with independent BLS employment projections.

Adaptability scores follow GovAI / Brookings methodology (Manning & Aguirre, 2026): education level, varied work experience, worker age, savings, and local labor market strength.

Trend arrows reflect directional movement in observed exposure from 2024 to 2026 per Anthropic Index updates, Goldman Sachs Research (March 2026), and PwC AI Jobs Barometer (2025). ↑ = exposure growing measurably; → = stable; ↓ = augmentation is increasing relative to automation (net positive for workers). Dot size = approximate US employment (BLS 2025 estimates).
