How this data was compiled The Washington Post's interactive drew on a 2026 study by Sam Manning (GovAI) and Tomás Aguirre, assessed by Brookings senior fellow Mark Muro. Researchers started with an industry-standard AI exposure score — measuring how many of each occupation's tasks (from the O*NET database) could plausibly be performed more efficiently with AI tools. They then layered in an adaptability index accounting for factors including education level, varied work experience, worker age, personal savings, and local labor market strength. The combination reveals not just who faces disruption, but who has pathways out. Separately, Anthropic researchers analyzed 1 million Claude conversations to map automation vs. augmentation across 700+ occupations.
