What is Google Gemini?

Google Gemini is Google's flagship generative AI model and chatbot, designed to understand and generate human-like text, images, and other content. It's built to be multimodal, meaning it can understand and work with text, images, video, audio, and code all together.

Current Versions (2025)

  • Gemini 2.5 Pro: The most intelligent AI model with enhanced reasoning capabilities
  • Gemini 2.5 Flash: The most efficient workhorse model designed for speed and low-cost
  • Gemini Live: Interactive voice conversations with real-time responses

Key Capabilities

Text Generation

Create lesson plans, emails, assignments, and administrative documents

Image Generation

Create visual aids and illustrations for presentations

Education Subscriptions

  1. Sign in with your Google account
  2. Review privacy settings - choose whether to save chat history
  3. Explore the interface - familiarize yourself with the chat window and tools
  4. Test basic functionality with simple questions

Hands-On Activities

Activity 1: Creating a Lesson Plan (15 minutes)

Your Task: Create a lesson plan for a topic you teach.

Step-by-Step:

  1. Go to gemini.google.com
  2. Use this prompt template:
Create a 50-minute lesson plan for [YOUR SUBJECT] on [SPECIFIC TOPIC]. Include: - Learning objectives - Opening hook (5 minutes) - Main activities (35 minutes) - Closing/assessment (10 minutes) - Required materials My students are [DESCRIPTION OF STUDENTS] and their skill level is [BEGINNER/INTERMEDIATE/ADVANCED].
  1. Review and refine the output
  2. Ask follow-up questions to improve specific sections

Activity 2: Email Communication (10 minutes)

Your Task: Draft a professional email for a common scenario.

Example Prompts:

  • "Draft an email to faculty about upcoming curriculum changes"
  • "Write a response to a student requesting a deadline extension"
  • "Create an email inviting guest speakers to campus"

Activity 3: Data Analysis (10 minutes)

Your Task: Upload a document or paste data for analysis.

Steps:

  1. Upload a document or paste text data
  2. Ask Gemini to summarize key points
  3. Request specific insights or trends
  4. Ask for recommendations based on the data

Activity 4: Creative Content Generation (10 minutes)

Your Task: Create visual or interactive content.

Example Prompts:

  • "Create an infographic concept about study strategies"
  • "Generate discussion questions for online forum participation"
  • "Design a quiz format for student engagement"

Best Practices

Effective Prompting Techniques

1. Be Specific

Include context, audience, and desired format

  • ❌ "Help with teaching"
  • ✅ "Create a rubric for evaluating group presentations in a business communication course"

2. Provide Context

Share relevant details about your students and institution

Example: "My students are first-generation college students with varying academic backgrounds"

3. Iterate and Refine

Use follow-up questions to improve outputs

  • "Make this more concise"
  • "Add examples for each main point"
  • "Adapt this for visual learners"

4. Request Multiple Options

Ask for alternatives to choose from

"Give me three different approaches to teaching this concept"

Quality Control

Important Reminders:

  • Always Review: AI-generated content should always be reviewed for accuracy and appropriateness
  • Fact-Check: Use Gemini's double-check feature to verify information
  • Customize: Adapt generated content to your specific context and students
  • Maintain Your Voice: Edit content to match your teaching style

Privacy and Security

Data Protection Features

  • Enterprise-Grade Security: Gemini for Google Workspace provides enterprise-grade data protection
  • FERPA Compliance: Google Workspace for Education meets FERPA, GDPR, and COPPA standards
  • Data Control: Customer data is not used to train AI models without permission

Privacy Settings

  1. Chat History: Choose whether to save conversations
  2. Data Sharing: Control how your data is used
  3. Account Settings: Review and adjust privacy preferences regularly

Best Practices for Sensitive Information

  • Avoid sharing student personal information
  • Use generic examples and replace real names with placeholders
  • Review outputs to ensure they don't contain sensitive information
  • Follow your college's AI and data privacy policies

Common Challenges and Solutions

Challenge 1: Generic Outputs

Solution: Provide more specific context and examples in your prompts

Challenge 2: Factual Errors

Solution: Always use the double-check feature and verify information independently

Challenge 3: Inappropriate Content

Solution: Review all outputs before using with students or in professional settings

Challenge 4: Over-Reliance

Solution: Use AI as a starting point, not a replacement for professional judgment
