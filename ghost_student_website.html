What is the "Ghost Student" Scheme?

The "Ghost Student" scheme is a sophisticated fraud where criminals use stolen or fake identities to enroll in colleges, apply for financial aid, and then disappear with the money without ever attending classes.

🧬 What is Synthetic Identity Fraud?

Also known as "Frankenstein fraud," synthetic identity fraud involves constructing a fictitious identity that includes both authentic and fabricated details, such as:

  • Pairing a real Social Security number (SSN) with a fake name
  • Pairing a real SSN and the real name of a different individual
  • Combining a fake SSN with a real name

How the Scheme Works:

  1. Identity Theft: Criminals use stolen Social Security numbers, fake names, or deceased individuals' information through synthetic identity fraud
  2. Easy Targets: They focus on open-access community colleges with less scrutiny
  3. Transfer Students: Often pose as transfer students to avoid additional verification
  4. Financial Aid Fraud: Apply for grants, loans, and scholarships worth up to $20,000 per "student"
  5. System Access: Gain access to campus networks, email, and cloud storage for additional scams
  6. Disappear: Never attend classes, keeping all the financial aid money

Red Flags to Watch For

If you work at a college or are a student, be aware of these suspicious activities:

  • 🌐 IP Address Patterns: Multiple students using the same internet addresses during registration or login
  • 💳 Financial Account Sharing: Multiple students providing identical bank accounts for financial aid deposits
  • ⏰ Last-Minute Changes: Students changing financial account information right before aid disbursement
  • 📱 Shared Personal Info: Multiple students using same phone numbers, emails, or addresses
  • 📚 Mismatched Classes: Students enrolling in courses outside their declared major
  • 🗺️ Geographic Mismatches: Account access from locations far from listed addresses
  • 🌍 Foreign Access: Students accessing accounts from non-US IP addresses or VPNs
  • 📈 Enrollment Spikes: Unusual increases in enrollment, especially transfer students
  • 👤 Age Anomalies: Student applicants with unusual age ranges for their programs
  • ❌ Non-Participation: Student accounts marked with zero class participation

How to Protect Yourself and Your Institution

🎓 For Students

  • Never share your Social Security number, student ID, or financial aid information
  • Report suspicious enrollment patterns you notice
  • Verify financial aid communications through official channels
  • Monitor your financial aid account regularly
  • Be cautious of unusual requests for personal information

🏫 For Institutions

  • Train admissions and financial aid staff on fraud indicators
  • Implement automated fraud detection systems
  • Require official government-issued ID verification
  • Use commercial data verification solutions
  • Establish safeguards for financial aid approval processes

🔒 Technical Safeguards

  • Monitor IP address patterns during registration
  • Flag multiple accounts with shared information
  • Implement geographic location verification
  • Track VPN and proxy usage
  • Monitor class participation rates

📋 Verification Practices

  • Verify Social Security numbers with official databases
  • Cross-reference personal information
  • Confirm transfer student credentials
  • Validate financial account ownership
  • Review unusual enrollment patterns

Report Suspected Ghost Student Fraud

If you suspect ghost student fraud at your institution or have information about these schemes, report it immediately.

What to Include in Your Report:

  • Details of suspicious enrollment patterns
  • Financial losses or attempted fraud
  • Timeline of events
  • Any evidence of identity theft
  • Contact information for follow-up
