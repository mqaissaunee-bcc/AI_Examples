Understanding What Engineers Do

üìñ Lesson 1.1: What is Engineering?

‚ñº

Engineering is the application of science, mathematics, and creativity to solve real-world problems. Engineers design, build, and improve systems, structures, devices, and processes that make our lives better, safer, and more efficient.

The Engineering Mindset

Successful engineers share common traits:

  • Problem Solvers ‚Äî Engineers see challenges as opportunities to create solutions
  • Analytical Thinkers ‚Äî They break complex problems into manageable parts
  • Creative Designers ‚Äî Engineering requires imagination to envision new possibilities
  • Team Players ‚Äî Modern engineering projects require collaboration across disciplines
  • Lifelong Learners ‚Äî Technology evolves rapidly, requiring continuous education
üí° From "The Existential Pleasures of Engineering" by Samuel C. Florman

"Engineering is often perceived as cold and void of passion, but this book demonstrates the profession's deep and rich rewards. Engineering is almost a primal instinct, and engineers build things with humanity in mind."

Engineering in Daily Life

Everything around you was touched by engineering:

  • Your smartphone (electrical, software, mechanical engineering)
  • The building you're in (civil, structural engineering)
  • Clean water from your tap (environmental, civil engineering)
  • The car or bus you take (mechanical, automotive engineering)
  • Medical devices (biomedical engineering)

üìñ Lesson 2.1: Educational Requirements

Typical Education Path

High School

Focus on math (algebra, calculus, trigonometry), physics, chemistry, and computer science. Strong foundation is essential.

Community College (Associate's Degree)

Complete first two years of engineering curriculum. Transfer to 4-year university. Brookdale offers A.S. in Engineering.

Bachelor's Degree (B.S.)

4-year degree from an ABET-accreditedABET (Accreditation Board for Engineering and Technology) is the recognized accreditor of college programs in engineering. Employers and licensing boards require graduation from ABET-accredited programs. program. Required for most engineering positions.

Master's Degree (Optional)

Advanced degree for specialization or leadership roles. May reduce PE experience requirements.

Doctorate (Ph.D.)

Required for research positions and university teaching.

üìñ Lesson 2.2: Professional Licensing (PE)

‚ñº

The Professional Engineer (PE)A PE is an engineer who has earned a license to practice engineering. Only PEs can sign and seal engineering plans and offer services directly to the public. The license demonstrates competence, ethics, and dedication to public safety. license is the highest standard of competence for engineers.

Path to PE Licensure

1. Graduate from ABET-Accredited Program

Earn a bachelor's degree in engineering from an accredited school.

2. Pass the FE Exam

The Fundamentals of Engineering (FE)A 110-question, 6-hour computer-based exam testing basic engineering knowledge. Typically taken during senior year or shortly after graduation. Passing makes you an Engineer Intern (EI) or Engineer-in-Training (EIT). exam is typically taken during your senior year.

3. Gain Experience

Work 4 years under the supervision of a licensed PE.

4. Pass the PE Exam

The Principles and Practice of Engineering (PE) exam tests advanced competence in your discipline.

‚ö†Ô∏è Why Get Licensed?
  • Required to offer engineering services to the public
  • Required to sign and seal engineering documents
  • Higher earning potential
  • Career mobility and advancement
  • Protection against outsourcing
  • Credibility with clients and employers

Technical and Professional Competencies

üìñ Lesson 3.1: Technical Skills

Core Technical Competencies

  • Mathematics ‚Äî Calculus, differential equations, linear algebra, statistics
  • Physics ‚Äî Mechanics, thermodynamics, electromagnetism, materials science
  • Computer Skills ‚Äî CAD software (AutoCAD, SolidWorks), programming (Python, MATLAB, C++)

Mechanical Engineering

üìñ Lesson 3.2: Professional (Soft) Skills

‚ñº

Technical skills alone won't make you successful. Employers consistently rank these professional skills as critical:

  • Communication ‚Äî Present complex technical information to non-technical audiences. Write clear reports and proposals.
  • Teamwork ‚Äî Collaborate effectively with diverse teams across departments and disciplines.
  • Problem Solving ‚Äî Apply systematic approaches to analyze and solve complex problems.
  • Project Management ‚Äî Plan, organize, and execute projects within budget and schedule constraints.
  • Leadership ‚Äî Guide teams, mentor junior engineers, and take responsibility for outcomes.
  • Ethics ‚Äî Make decisions that protect public safety and welfare.

Module 4: Launching Your Engineering Career

Internships, Resumes, and Job Searching

‚è±Ô∏è 50 min

üìñ Lesson 4.1: Gaining Experience

‚ñº

Ways to Build Experience

  • Internships ‚Äî The most valuable experience. Many companies hire interns into full-time positions.
  • Co-op Programs ‚Äî Alternate semesters of work and school for deeper experience.
  • Research Projects ‚Äî Work with professors on research. Great for graduate school applications.
  • Engineering Clubs ‚Äî Join SAE, ASCE, IEEE student chapters. Participate in competitions.
  • Personal Projects ‚Äî Build things! Document on GitHub or a portfolio website.
üí° Pro Tip

Start applying for internships early in your education. Many companies recruit 6-12 months in advance. Even freshman and sophomore internships exist!

üìñ Lesson 4.2: Building Your Resume

‚ñº

Engineering Resume Essentials

  • Contact Information ‚Äî Name, phone, professional email, LinkedIn, portfolio link
  • Education ‚Äî Degree, school, GPA (if above 3.0), relevant coursework
  • Technical Skills ‚Äî Software, programming languages, lab equipment
  • Projects ‚Äî Class projects, personal projects, competition entries
  • Experience ‚Äî Internships, co-ops, research, part-time jobs (highlight transferable skills)
  • Leadership ‚Äî Club officers, team captain, mentoring roles

Resume Power Words for Engineers

Use action verbs: Designed, Analyzed, Developed, Implemented, Optimized, Tested, Collaborated, Led, Programmed, Calculated, Simulated, Documented

‚ö†Ô∏è Common Mistakes to Avoid
  • Generic objectives‚Äîbe specific about your target role
  • No quantifiable results‚Äîinclude metrics when possible
  • Typos or formatting errors‚Äîhave others review
  • Too long‚Äîkeep to 1 page for students
