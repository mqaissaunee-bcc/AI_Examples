Session Details

This foundational session introduces essential AI concepts and addresses common concerns about AI adoption in the classroom. We'll explore key terminology including tokens and how AI processes language, discuss strategies for overcoming AI resistance among faculty and students, and examine how educators can approach AI thoughtfully and ethically.

Topics Covered:
  • Terminology (Tokens)
  • AI Resistance
  • Critical Thinkers
  • Routine Tasks

Learn how to leverage AI for creating and enhancing instructional materials while maintaining educational quality and academic standards. This hands-on session covers syllabus development, lesson planning, engaging activity design, and AI course policies that promote responsible use while preserving learning objectives.

Topics Covered:
  • Syllabus
  • Lesson Plans
  • Activities
  • AI Course Policy

Explore AI's capabilities in multimedia creation and enhancement for educational purposes. Discover tools and techniques for generating educational podcasts, creating and editing instructional videos, and producing images that enrich course materials, accommodate diverse learning styles, and engage students in new ways.

Topics Covered:
  • Podcasts
  • Video
  • Images

The final session delves into advanced AI technologies including agentic systems that can perform complex, multi-step tasks to support teaching and student services. Explore agentic browsers for curriculum research and agent chatbots that provide sophisticated, context-aware assistance for instructional design and student support.

Topics Covered:
  • Agentic Browsers
  • Agent Chatbots
