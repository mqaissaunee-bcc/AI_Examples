New to case studies? Start here.

This is a short on-ramp to the Case Study Prompt Builder. Answer five questions about your discipline and what you want students to do. The wizard will prepare a starting configuration and hand it off — you'll land on the builder with sensible defaults already filled in, ready to refine. Already know exactly what you want? Skip the wizard and open the builder directly.

1What do you teach?

Free text. Used verbatim in the case study (e.g., "Registered Nursing," "Business Ethics," "U.S. History," "Automotive Technology").

2What do you want students to do?

Pick the closest match. This shapes everything: the scenario fields, the artifact library, and the section labels in the generated case study.

3What level are your students?

Shapes language complexity, scaffolding, and how much jargon the case explains.

4How long should it take?

In-class activity, full session, or a multi-session project — choose the rough duration.

5Describe the case in one or two sentences.

This becomes the opening "narrative hook" the student reads. Be specific — what's happening, who's involved, what's at stake.
