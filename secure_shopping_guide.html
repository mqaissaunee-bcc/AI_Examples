üö© Spot the Red Flags

‚ö†Ô∏è Warning Signs of Fake Online Stores

  • Deals that are significantly cheaper than established retailers (70%+ off)
  • No contact information or only personal email addresses
  • Missing or unclear return policies
  • Suspicious domain names (e.g., "aamazon.deals" instead of "amazon.com")
  • No customer reviews or only positive reviews from new accounts
  • Only accepts wire transfers, gift cards, or cryptocurrency
  • Poor grammar or spelling throughout the website
  • Website looks identical to a known retailer but with a different URL

‚úÖ Shopping Security Best Practices

üè™

Shop Trusted Retailers

Buy from online stores you know and have used before. Bookmark your favorite retailers in your browser to avoid fake look-alike sites.

üîç

Examine Web Addresses

Check the URL carefully! Look for misspellings or unusual domain extensions. Legitimate sites use HTTPS (look for the padlock üîí).

üìù

Search for Reviews

Google the store name with terms like "scam," "fraud," or "reviews." Check multiple sources before making a purchase.

üìû

Verify Contact Details

Legitimate businesses have physical addresses, phone numbers, and customer service contacts. Test them before buying!

üîê

Secure Your Accounts

Use unique, strong passwords for each site. Enable multi-factor authentication (MFA) and use a password manager.

‚≠ê

Check Seller Reputation

On marketplace sites (Amazon, eBay), verify seller ratings, reviews, and how long they've been selling.

üí≥ Payment Method Safety Guide

üí∞

Enable Alerts

Set up text or email notifications for all charges on your credit card. Catch fraud immediately!

üìä

Review your credit card statements regularly. Report suspicious charges to your bank immediately.

Use Virtual Cards

Many credit cards offer virtual card numbers for online shopping. They can be frozen or canceled without affecting your main card.

üÜò If You've Been Scammed

  1. Contact your bank/credit card company immediately to report fraud and stop payments
  2. Change your passwords for all accounts, especially if you reused passwords
  3. File a report with the FTC at reportfraud.ftc.gov
  4. Monitor your credit for suspicious activity in the coming months
  5. Document everything - save emails, screenshots, and receipts
