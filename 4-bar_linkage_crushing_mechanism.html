What the plot shows

The blue curve is the ram's displacement; the rose curve is its velocity (dx/dθ). Because the crank pivot sits above the slider line, this is an offset slider‑crank — notice the curve is not symmetric: the forward (power) stroke and the return stroke take different fractions of the revolution. That asymmetry is the quick‑return effect. Velocity crosses zero at the two dead‑center positions, exactly where crushing force peaks.

Drag Crank r and Rod L to explore the L/r ratio: a small ratio exaggerates the asymmetry and the velocity spikes, while a large ratio makes the motion approach a clean sinusoid. The boulder and anvil reposition automatically to the ram's full reach so crushing always lands on the power stroke.
