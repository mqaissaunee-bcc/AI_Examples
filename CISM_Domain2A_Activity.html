The Scenario

Company: FinSecure, a rapidly growing financial services firm known for its online investment platform for high-net-worth individuals. The company is currently in a "quiet period" preparing for a major IPO in six months.

The Situation: It's 8:00 AM on Monday, July 28, 2025. The CISO receives a high-priority alert from their threat intelligence provider and an official warning from CISA. A state-sponsored threat actor, known for targeting Western financial institutions, has released a new ransomware variant called "LockChain." LockChain is a double-extortion tool: it exfiltrates sensitive customer and financial data before encrypting critical systems. The attackers are known to publish the data of non-paying victims.

The attack vector is a critical Remote Code Execution (RCE) vulnerability (CVE-2025-12345) in the third-party "VeriData API" used by FinSecure for real-time customer data validation. Your logs show the API is internet-facing. Making matters worse, Alex, a senior network engineer with high-level administrative privileges, was abruptly terminated last Friday after a heated performance dispute. A routine exit audit shows one of his assigned corporate laptops is unaccounted for.
