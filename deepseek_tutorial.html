What is DeepSeek?

DeepSeek is a Chinese AI startup founded in 2023 that has disrupted the AI industry with breakthrough models that rival OpenAI's GPT-4 and o1 at a fraction of the cost. While technically impressive, DeepSeek raises significant security and privacy concerns for educational use.

⚡ Key Technical Features

  • 🧠Advanced Reasoning: Chain-of-thought processing similar to OpenAI's o1 model
  • 💰Cost Efficiency: Priced at 1/30th the cost of comparable OpenAI models
  • 🔓Open Source: Available under MIT license for research and commercial use
  • 🔍Large Context: 128,000 token context window for processing long documents
  • 👁️Multimodal: Vision capabilities for image understanding and generation
  • 💻Multiple Access: Web interface, mobile apps, and API access available
  • ⚠️Limited Safety: Lacks robust safety guardrails compared to Western AI models

🔴 Critical Limitations

  • Censorship: Cannot discuss sensitive topics like Tiananmen Square or Taiwan sovereignty
  • Security Vulnerabilities: 100% attack success rate in security testing
  • Data Location: All user data stored on servers in China
  • Government Access: Subject to Chinese data sovereignty laws

⚠️ Important Considerations for Educators

While DeepSeek offers impressive technical capabilities, educational institutions must carefully weigh these benefits against significant security, privacy, and compliance risks. Many universities and government agencies have chosen to prohibit its use.

Getting Started (With Caution)

🎯 Access Methods

Available Platforms

  • Web Interface: Direct access through DeepSeek's website
  • Mobile Apps: iOS and Android applications (banned in some regions)
  • API Access: Developer integration for applications
  • Open Source: Self-hosted deployment via Hugging Face (requires significant resources)
  • Cloud Platforms: Available through Amazon Web Services Bedrock

🔧 Basic Setup Process

  1. Risk Assessment: Review your institution's AI policies and data protection requirements
  2. Account Creation: Sign up through website (avoid linking Google/Apple accounts)
  3. Privacy Settings: Review and understand data collection practices
  4. Network Security: Consider using VPN if accessing for evaluation purposes

Security Recommendation: Before using DeepSeek, consult with your institution's IT security team and legal counsel. Many educational institutions have policies prohibiting the use of AI tools that store data in countries with mandatory government access laws.

Security Concerns and Privacy Risks

🚨 Critical Security Issues

Security research has identified severe vulnerabilities in DeepSeek:

  • 100% Attack Success Rate: Failed to block any harmful prompts in security testing
  • No Safety Guardrails: Lacks robust protections against malicious use
  • Algorithmic Jailbreaking: Highly susceptible to security bypass techniques
  • Unencrypted Data Transmission: Device information sent without encryption

🔒 Data Privacy Concerns

Data Collection

  • Extensive device fingerprinting
  • Keystroke patterns and rhythms
  • Chat messages and personal information
  • Network and usage data
  • Location and browsing activity

Data Storage

  • All data stored on servers in China
  • Subject to Chinese data sovereignty laws
  • Government access without legal process
  • No GDPR or FERPA protections
  • Indefinite data retention

Third-Party Connections

  • Links to ByteDance/TikTok infrastructure
  • China Mobile network connections
  • Undisclosed data sharing partners
  • Advertising and analytics networks
  • User deanonymization risks

⚖️ Legal and Compliance Issues

Regulatory Violations

  • FERPA: Student education records potentially exposed to foreign government
  • GDPR: European data protection laws circumvented
  • State Privacy Laws: Various U.S. state regulations violated
  • Institutional Policies: Many universities prohibit foreign data storage
  • Export Controls: Potential violations of technology transfer restrictions

Risk Assessment Activities

Activity 1: Security Risk Evaluation (30 minutes)

Your Task: Conduct a thorough risk assessment for your institution.

Evaluation Framework:

  1. Review your institution's data classification policies
  2. Identify what types of data faculty and staff typically process
  3. Assess compliance requirements (FERPA, state privacy laws, etc.)
  4. Evaluate potential reputational and legal risks
  5. Compare with approved AI tools already in use
Questions to Consider: - What student data might inadvertently be shared with DeepSeek? - How would data exposure to a foreign government affect our compliance status? - What are the potential consequences if our institution's research or strategies are accessed? - Do we have adequate incident response procedures for data breaches involving foreign entities?

Activity 2: Policy Development Workshop (45 minutes)

Your Task: Develop institutional guidelines for AI tool evaluation.

Policy Elements:

  1. Create criteria for evaluating AI tools
  2. Establish data sovereignty requirements
