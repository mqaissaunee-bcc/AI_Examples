墨流し · Suminagashi

The old Japanese art of floating ink on water

Drops of ink rest on still water and unfurl into rivers and woodgrain. Here the water is a live fluid simulation, and your pointer is the current. The ink mixes subtractively, the way real pigment darkens paper.

How to play

Tap to drop a bead of ink. Drag to pour a ribbon and stir the water — moving across existing ink drags it into swirls. Auto lets the surface paint itself when you step away. Wash rinses it clean.
