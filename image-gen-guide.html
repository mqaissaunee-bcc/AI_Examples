Key Terms Explained
Plain language for complete beginners.
Diffusion Model
The AI that generates images. It starts with noise and gradually "diffuses" it into a coherent image guided by your prompt.
Prompt
The text description of what you want. More specific = better results. "A golden retriever on a beach at sunset, oil painting style."
Negative Prompt
What you DON'T want. E.g., "blurry, low quality, extra limbs." Helps avoid common AI image artifacts.
CFG Scale
How strictly the AI follows your prompt. Low (1–5) = creative freedom. High (7–15) = sticks closely to your words.
Steps
How many denoising steps the AI takes. More steps = higher quality but slower. 20–30 is the sweet spot for most models.
Checkpoint / .safetensors
The model file you download. Each one has a different style — photorealism, anime, painting, etc. Mix and match.
LoRA
A small add-on file (Low-Rank Adaptation) that modifies an existing model. Use them to add specific styles, characters, or faces.
VAE
Variational Autoencoder — processes the image's color and detail. Using the right VAE sharpens colors and reduces washed-out results.
Sampler
The algorithm that guides each denoising step. DPM++ 2M Karras and Euler a are popular starting points.
VRAM
Video RAM on your GPU. More VRAM = bigger images and faster generation. 8 GB is comfortable; 4 GB works with limits.
Flux.1
A newer, high-quality image model from Black Forest Labs. Better prompt adherence than SD but requires more VRAM.
img2img
Give the AI a starting image instead of pure noise. Great for variations, style transfers, or fixing existing images.

FLUX.2 [dev] shipped Nov 2025; the distilled Klein models (9B and 4B) followed Jan 2026.
⚡ Fastest local
FLUX.2 Klein 4B · New 2026
Distilled for local use — fast, near-interactive generation. Apache 2.0 (commercial-friendly). Runs on ~12 GB VRAM. The best entry point to FLUX.2 on a mid-range card. Run via ComfyUI.
FLUX.2 Klein 9B · New 2026
A larger distilled variant — better quality than 4B while still local-friendly. Fits ~16 GB VRAM. Apache 2.0. Good balance of speed and fidelity.
⭐ Best Quality
FLUX.2 [dev] · New 2026
The full 32B model — top photorealism, strongest prompt adherence, best text. GGUF Q4 is ~19 GB and wants a 24 GB card; FP8 needs ~32 GB. Search Hugging Face: black-forest-labs/FLUX.2-dev.
⚙️FLUX settings differ from Stable Diffusion: set CFG (guidance) to 1.0, leave negative prompts empty (Flux ignores them), and use euler + simple as a safe sampler/scheduler. The old SD defaults (CFG 7, long negatives) will oversaturate Flux output.
ℹ️FLUX.2 also comes in Pro, Max, and Flex tiers — but those are API/cloud only. Only [dev] and Klein have downloadable weights you can run locally.

CFG Scale7How closely the AI follows your prompt. 7 is the classic sweet spot. Higher = more literal. Lower = more creative.
SamplerDPM++ 2M KarrasThe algorithm for each step. DPM++ 2M Karras is reliable and fast. Euler a is good for artistic/creative outputs.

Seed-1 (Random)-1 generates different images each time. Fix a seed to reproduce or vary a specific result.
Batch Size1Start with 1. Increase to generate multiple variations at once, but requires more VRAM.

⚙️Using Flux / FLUX.2? These defaults change. Set CFG (guidance) ≈ 1.0, leave negative prompts empty (Flux ignores them), use euler + simple, and ~20 steps (4–8 for Schnell/Klein). The classic CFG 7 + long negatives above are for Stable Diffusion models and will oversaturate Flux.

Prompt Writing Tips
How to get the image you actually want.
✅ Good Prompt Structure
[subject], [environment], [lighting], [style], [quality words]

"a golden retriever sitting in an autumn forest, dappled sunlight, oil painting style, highly detailed, 8k"
❌ Weak Prompt
"dog in forest"
💡 Power Words to Add
highly detailed · 8k · cinematic lighting · bokeh · sharp focus · award-winning photography · professional
💡Universal negative prompt to always use: blurry, low quality, bad anatomy, extra limbs, deformed hands, watermark, signature, text, ugly, worst quality

Common Problems & Fixes
❌Black or blank images: Wrong VAE, or VRAM overflow. Try disabling your VAE first. If still black, lower your resolution or use a smaller model.
❌CUDA out of memory: Image resolution or batch size too large for your VRAM. Drop to 512×512 and batch size 1. Try enabling xformers in A1111 (saves ~30% VRAM).
❌Distorted faces / extra fingers: Use a face restoration tool (A1111 has built-in ADetailer extension) and add bad hands, deformed fingers to your negative prompt.
⚠️Very slow generation: GPU acceleration may not be active. On A1111, check the console for "Running on CPU" — if so, reinstall with correct CUDA version for your driver.
⚠️Draw Things slow on Intel Mac: Intel Macs don't have Metal acceleration for AI. Expect 5–20 minutes per image. Smaller models help significantly.
💡Apple Silicon tip: Draw Things and ComfyUI both support Metal acceleration on M-series chips. Make sure you're using the Apple Silicon build — it's dramatically faster than CPU mode.
✅Best beginner settings: 20 steps · CFG 7 · DPM++ 2M Karras · 512×512 · batch 1 · seed -1. Get one image working before experimenting.

ControlNet — Guide the Composition
The most powerful technique for precise image control.

ControlNet is an add-on that lets you provide a reference image to guide the structure of the output — not just the style. Instead of hoping the AI places elements where you want, you can dictate exact poses, edges, depth maps, and layouts.

Pose Control
OpenPose
Provide a stick-figure pose sketch → the model generates a person in exactly that pose. Great for consistent character placement.
Edge / Structure
Canny / HED
Feed in an edge-detected version of any image → generates a new image with the same structural layout. Perfect for educational diagrams.
Depth
Depth Map
Preserves the 3D depth structure of a reference image. Useful for recomposing a scene in a different style while keeping spatial relationships.
Sketch → Image
Scribble
Turn a rough hand-drawn sketch into a full image. You draw the rough shapes; the AI fills in textures, lighting, and detail.
💡How to install: In Automatic1111, go to Extensions → Install from URL and paste the ControlNet extension repo. In ComfyUI, ControlNet nodes are built-in. Download ControlNet model weights separately from Hugging Face: search "lllyasviel/ControlNet-v1-1".

What Good Output Looks Like
Example prompts and what to expect — vs. common failure modes.

Your first few images may be disappointing. Here's what separates a good prompt from a poor one, and what common failure modes look like.

✅ Strong Prompts
Good — Photorealistic Portrait
"A 35mm portrait of a weathered lighthouse keeper, piercing blue eyes, salt-and-pepper beard, dramatic side lighting, shallow depth of field, Kodak Portra 400 film grain, highly detailed"
→ Expect: Sharp, atmospheric portrait with realistic skin texture, good lighting, cinematic feel. Steps: 25, CFG: 7, DPM++ 2M Karras.
Good — Educational Illustration
"A clean infographic-style illustration of the water cycle, showing evaporation, condensation, and precipitation, labeled diagram, flat design, teal and sky blue color palette, white background"
→ Expect: Clean, stylized diagram with good color. With older SD models the labels come out garbled; with FLUX.2 short labels are now legible. For dense text, add it in Canva or PowerPoint afterward.
Good — Artistic Scene
"A misty forest path at dawn, golden hour light filtering through ancient oak trees, oil painting style, impressionist brushwork, rich earth tones, highly detailed"
→ Expect: Painterly, atmospheric scene with good light. Artistic models (Deliberate, DreamShaper) excel here.
❌ Common Failure Modes
Failure — Too Vague
"a person"
→ Result: Random composition, unclear lighting, generic face, muddy colors. Add: environment, lighting, style, quality words, camera type.
Failure — Contradictory Instructions
"a photorealistic watercolor painting of an anime character"
→ Result: Confused output — the model tries to satisfy conflicting styles and fails at all of them. Pick one style direction.
Failure — Text in Image
"a street sign reading STOP"
→ Result: Garbled, unreadable letters. Older SD models can't render coherent text. Switch to FLUX.2 (or Flux.1 Dev) for legible labels, or add text in post-processing.

Responsible Use
Important for all users — especially educators.
⚠️No content filters: Local image generation has no built-in moderation. By default, models can generate explicit, violent, or otherwise harmful imagery if prompted. Be intentional about who has access to local image generation tools.
⚠️Copyright and artist consent: Many open-source models were trained on images scraped from the internet, often without artist consent. This is actively contested legally and ethically. Be transparent about how images were generated when sharing them.
⚠️Disclosure: AI-generated images can be indistinguishable from photographs. Always disclose when sharing AI-generated images, particularly in academic, journalistic, or professional contexts.
💡For classroom use: Consider using a fast model like FLUX.2 Klein, SD 3.5, or SDXL-Turbo for live demonstrations — the speed keeps a class moving, and the visible "obviously AI" tells in some outputs open productive conversations about AI image recognition and critical media literacy.
✅Safe exploration: For purely educational exploration, using artistic/stylized models (illustration, painting styles) rather than photorealistic ones reduces the risk of generating content that could be confused with real photographs of real people.

What's New & What's Coming
Recent developments and what to watch for.
✅FLUX.2 (Nov 2025 – Jan 2026) — Black Forest Labs' second generation. The headline wins: genuinely readable text in images, multi-reference editing (up to 10 images for consistent characters/products), ~4 MP output, and pose control. [dev] (24 GB) and the distilled Klein 4B/9B models run locally in ComfyUI. See the Flux & New Models tab above.
✅Local video generation is here — What used to be "coming soon" now runs on your own machine. ComfyUI supports Wan, HunyuanVideo, and Mochi for text-to-video and image-to-video. Quality and length are limited versus cloud tools (Runway, Sora), and it's VRAM-hungry, but free local video clips are real now.
⚡Faster, lighter models — A wave of "turbo" and distilled models (e.g. Z-Image Turbo, FLUX.2 Klein) target near-real-time generation on modest GPUs. If your card is mid-range, these close much of the speed gap with cloud services.
🧰Easier setup — Stability Matrix has become the popular one-click installer/manager — it installs ComfyUI or Forge, handles dependencies, and shares one model folder across tools. A much gentler on-ramp than manual installs.
💡You may also see: Ollama — Ollama is a command-line tool for running local text AI models (like those in LM Studio), not image generation. If a tutorial mentions Ollama, it's not about Stable Diffusion. See the companion LM Studio guide for that.
