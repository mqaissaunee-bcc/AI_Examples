Scoring rubric & methodology

Each occupation is scored on two dimensions, derived from a weighted rubric synthesizing BLS employment data, multiple AI-exposure research papers, and the recent empirical evidence on entry-level labor market disruption.

AI Vulnerability (0–100)

Sub-scoreWeightWhat it measures
Task displacement40%What share of the role's tasks current LLMs/agents can do unsupervised. Anchored to Eloundou et al. (2023) exposure + Anthropic Economic Index actual usage data.
Entry-level disruption30%Empirical evidence of hiring decline since 2022, especially for ages 22–25. Anchored to Brynjolfsson, Chandar & Chen (Aug 2025) "Canaries in the Coal Mine."
Physical / embodied work20%Inverted: how much of the job requires physical presence and manual skill. Physical work scores low here, reducing vulnerability.
Regulatory / licensure10%Inverted: does the role require a license, certification, or carry legal liability AI cannot bear.

AI Adaptability (0–100)

Sub-scoreWeightWhat it measures
Productivity leverage40%How much a worker who uses AI well can outperform one who doesn't. Anchored to Brynjolfsson, Li & Raymond (2025) "Generative AI at Work" and Anthropic Economic Index productivity findings.
AI tool ecosystem30%Whether domain-specific AI tools are well-developed today.
Skill transferability20%Whether workers can pivot toward adjacent AI-augmented work without leaving the field.
Career ceiling with AI fluency10%Whether adding AI skills raises earning potential.

Zone definitions

Roles are classified by combining the two scores plus the canary flag (empirical entry-level disruption):

  • AI Allied (Green): Low vulnerability (<35) + high adaptability (≥70). AI helps far more than it threatens.
  • Evolving (Yellow): Mixed signals — these roles are changing significantly. Includes high-vulnerability roles with strong adaptability rescue (e.g., AI/Data Science roles where the work itself is AI-native).
  • Starting Line Shifted (Red — canary): High vulnerability + documented entry-level hiring decline. The career still exists, but the path in has changed. Student guidance focuses on how to still get hired.
  • Under Pressure (Red — no canary): High vulnerability without strong adaptability rescue. Career evolution required.
  • Steady Ground (Stable): Low vulnerability + limited AI upside. Reliable work; pay growth comes from skill specialization, not AI fluency.

NJ overlay

The "New Jersey" toggle applies per-program multipliers grounded in BLS NY-Newark-Jersey City MSA and NJ statewide OEWS May 2024 data. Multipliers range from 1.05 for trades to 1.20 for business/finance (NJ has strong financial sector concentration). For Brookdale's service area (Monmouth County), the NY-NJ MSA reflects local wages well.

Limitations

Scores represent informed synthesis, not measurements. Reasonable people will disagree on individual scores; the rubric is published so disagreements can be specific (which sub-score weight is wrong) rather than vague. Salary and growth data represent national averages; individual outcomes vary substantially by employer, specialization, and experience. The Brynjolfsson "Canaries" finding is recent and may not generalize — it's the strongest empirical signal we have, but it's one study.
