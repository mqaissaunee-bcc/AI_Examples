AI Impact on Career Paths

AI Vulnerability (0–100)

How much of this job's core tasks could be automated by AI. Higher scores mean more daily work is at risk of replacement by AI tools, software, or robotics.

AI Adaptability (0–100)

How well workers in this role can leverage AI to become more productive and valuable. Higher scores mean greater potential to work with AI rather than be replaced by it.

Why Both Matter

A job can score high on both scales. Software developers, for example, face high vulnerability (AI writes code) but also high adaptability (developers using AI tools are far more productive). The combination tells the real story.

Green Zone
Low vulnerability, high adaptability. Best positioned — AI helps more than it threatens.
Yellow Zone
Mixed signals. These roles are evolving — upskilling is essential to stay ahead.
Red Zone
High vulnerability, low adaptability. Most at risk — consider adjacent career paths.
Stable Zone
Low vulnerability, low adaptability. Steady work, but limited AI upside.

Methodology

This dashboard uses a two-dimensional model: Vulnerability (0-100) measures task automation risk based on job task composition, with scores derived from multiple academic studies. Adaptability (0-100) measures AI leverage potential—how effectively workers can use AI tools to enhance productivity. Composite scores synthesize findings from Eloundou et al., Frey-Osborne, and other cited research. Where specific occupations aren't tracked by BLS or AI research, the closest matching SOC code or occupational proxy is used. All salary and growth data represent national averages and vary significantly by geography, employer, experience level, and specialization.
