What is AI Resistance Studio?

AI Resistance Studio is a standalone browser-based tool that helps faculty add targeted, pedagogically grounded resistance requirements to any assignment — essays, projects, discussion posts, lab reports, presentations, and more.

You paste in your assignment text, configure a few options, and the Studio generates a strengthened version that adds explicit requirements making the assignment harder to complete meaningfully with AI alone. Nothing is installed, no account is needed, and your text never leaves your browser.

🛡️ What "resistance" means here AI Resistance Studio doesn't try to detect AI use — detection tools are unreliable and punish students unfairly. Instead, it strengthens the assignment design so that authentic human effort is genuinely required and clearly visible in the submission. The goal is better pedagogy, not surveillance.

1

Paste Your Assignment

The first thing you do is paste — or type — your existing assignment text into the large input area on the left side of the screen. This can be the assignment instructions as you'd currently share them with students, a draft you're still working on, or even just a rough description of what you want students to do.

The Studio works with what you give it. More specific input generally produces more targeted resistance requirements, but even a one-sentence description will generate useful output.

Your original text is always preserved in the output — the Studio appends resistance requirements after it rather than rewriting what you wrote. You'll see your original wording exactly as entered at the top of the output panel.

2

Attach a Reference Document

Just below the assignment textarea you'll find a dashed-border toggle: I have a reference document for this assignment. This is optional — use it when you have a rubric, a reading, a case study, a dataset, a syllabus excerpt, or any file you want the AI to treat as primary source material when students use the generated prompt.

📎 Reference Document Toggle
📎 Reference document will be included in prompt ✓
📎 ENG101_rubric.pdf selected
The strengthened prompt will instruct the AI to treat this document as the primary reference material. You'll upload the file when you paste the prompt into your AI tool.

When this toggle is active, the generated prompt includes a Reference Document section with instructions telling the AI to:

  • Treat the attached file as primary source material
  • Ground its response in the document's content, not just general knowledge
  • Follow rubric or prompt instructions if present, or analyze the subject matter content if that's what the file contains
📌 Important: two-step process The Studio generates a prompt you'll paste into an AI tool. Selecting a file here tells the Studio to include document-handling instructions in that prompt. You then upload the actual file in your AI tool (Claude, ChatGPT, etc.) alongside the copied prompt — just as you would attach any file when chatting with an AI. The Studio itself doesn't process or read the file.

Accepted file types include PDF, Word (.doc/.docx), PowerPoint (.pptx), Excel (.xlsx), plain text (.txt), and Rich Text (.rtf). If no file is selected but the toggle is on, the prompt will refer to "the file you will upload" as a placeholder.

3

Set Your AI Use Policy (AIAS Level)

The AIAS Scale (AI Integration and Accountability Scale) lets you specify how much AI use is permitted on this assignment. This choice directly affects the AI Use Policy language that gets injected into the output — students will see a clear, level-appropriate statement about what's allowed.

AIAS Level — How much AI should be permitted?
1No AI
2AI-Assisted
3AI-Integrated
4AI-Collaborative
5AI-Enhanced
Level 3 — AI-Integrated: AI acts as a collaboration tool. Students must explain their decision-making and submit process artifacts alongside the finished product.

The default is Level 3. Here's a quick reference for all five levels:

LevelNameWhat it permitsBest for
1 No AI No AI tools at any stage. Work must be entirely the student's own. In-class exams, handwritten work, foundational skill checks
2 AI-Assisted Brainstorming, grammar checking, and source-finding only. Attribution required. Introductory research tasks, teaching responsible AI use
3 AI-Integrated AI as a collaboration partner. Decision-making process artifacts required alongside the final product. Most standard writing and analysis assignments
4 AI-Collaborative AI-generated content is a permitted starting point. Assessment focuses on curation, revision, and synthesis. Advanced composition, editorial judgment tasks
5 AI-Enhanced Full AI integration expected. Metacognitive reflection on AI's role is required. AI literacy courses, experimental or exploratory assignments
Note: The AIAS level you choose doesn't change which resistance strategies are available — it only controls the AI Use Policy text in the output. You can combine any AIAS level with any strategy.

Here's a detailed breakdown of what each strategy adds to your assignment:

🎯 Highly Personalized +20 pts
Requires students to ground their work in their own specific context — workplace, clinical setting, community, or lived experience. Generic responses are explicitly disqualified.
Best for: essays, case analyses, reflection assignments, nursing/clinical work
🔗 Multilayer / Scaffolded +18 pts
Breaks the assignment into sequential stages: outline, first draft, revised final submission, and a revision narrative explaining what changed and why. Skipping stages is explicitly not permitted.
Best for: research papers, major projects, writing-intensive courses
📍 Local & Community Context +20 pts
Anchors the work in a real, local organization, community, or regional case the student can directly access. Requires at least one primary source interaction — an interview, site visit, or local document.
Best for: social sciences, business, public health, community college context
🧑 Personal Experience +16 pts
Connects course concepts to a specific situation the student has personally experienced — not a hypothetical. Includes a metacognitive reflection component asking what the assignment revealed about their own assumptions.
Best for: humanities, education, counseling, leadership courses
🎤 Beyond What AI Can Do +22 pts
Adds a live or oral component — a presentation, in-class defense, or real-time demonstration — where students will be asked unscripted follow-up questions. Cannot be pre-scripted or AI-generated.
Best for: capstone courses, seminars, any assignment where depth of understanding matters
🔄 Process Over Product +16 pts
Shifts grading weight (40–60%) to a process portfolio: brainstorming artifacts, annotated bibliography, intermediate drafts, and decision log. A polished final product with no process documentation won't earn full credit.
Best for: writing courses, research methods, any assignment where thinking matters as much as output
💡 Combining 2–3 strategies is the sweet spot A single strategy adds useful friction but is still possible to work around with careful prompting. Two or three complementary strategies — especially one that requires something personal or live — create assignments where AI assistance becomes a genuine liability rather than a shortcut.

Process Emphasis Slider

Below the strategy cards is the Process Emphasis slider — a five-point scale from Product-Focused (left) to Process-Heavy (right). This controls how much weight process documentation carries in the output, separate from the Process Over Product strategy card.

Process Emphasis
Product-FocusedBalancedProcess-Heavy
Balanced — equal weight on process and product

The slider defaults to position 3 (Balanced). Here's what each level means:

LevelLabelEffect on output
1Product-FocusedNo additional process language added. Output emphasizes the final product only.
2Lightly Process-AwareSuggests process documentation without making it required.
3BalancedDefault. Equal weight on process and product in the framing.
4Process-EmphasizedAdds a process documentation requirement section unless the Process Over Product strategy card is already active.
5Process-HeavyStrong process portfolio requirements added — brainstorming artifacts, annotated bibliography, decision log, and a final reflection. Carries significant grading weight.
Note: If you select the Process Over Product strategy card and set the slider to level 4 or 5, the card's requirements take precedence and no duplicate process section is added. The slider's levels 4–5 only fire when the strategy card is not active.
6

Add Discipline Context

The Discipline / Subject Area field is optional but recommended. Enter your discipline — Nursing, Business, English Composition, CTE, Psychology, History, whatever fits — and the Studio adds a discipline context paragraph to the output instructing students to use field-appropriate examples, sources, and analytical conventions.

Discipline / Subject Area (optional)
Nursing
Discipline context framing added for Nursing. Students will be directed to use field-appropriate examples, sources, and conventions.

This field is free-text — type anything. The output uses whatever you enter verbatim in a sentence like: "This assignment is situated within Nursing. Your examples, sources, and analysis should reflect the standards, conventions, and real-world contexts of this field."

Why this matters: Discipline framing makes AI-generated generic responses easier to spot. A student in a Nursing course drawing on their clinical placements will produce very different work than a generic AI response about healthcare. Adding discipline context makes that expectation explicit.

Resistance Score — The percentage shown at the top of the output panel is a composite score based on which strategies are active, your AIAS level, and the process slider position. It reflects potential resistance, not a guarantee: the score is only as meaningful as how you enforce the requirements in your grading.

Copy Full Prompt — Copies the complete strengthened output to your clipboard — your original assignment plus all injected resistance sections. Paste this wherever you share assignments: Canvas, a Word document, email, or directly into an AI tool.

Copy Original — Copies just your unmodified input text. Useful if you want to start over or share the original alongside the strengthened version.

What Was Added — A bulleted summary of every element the Studio injected. Use this as a checklist when updating your assignment in Canvas to make sure nothing is missing.

Start Over — Clears all fields and resets all options to defaults. The output panel returns to its placeholder state.

🛡️ Using the output in your course The strengthened output is a direct instruction block you can paste into your assignment description in Canvas, a syllabus, or a Word document. Some faculty paste just the injected sections (below the dotted line) and keep their original assignment language separate. Others paste the full output as the complete assignment prompt. Both approaches work — choose whatever fits your course formatting.
★

Strategy Combinations That Work Well

Some strategy pairings are especially effective together. Here are tested combinations for common assignment types:

Assignment TypeRecommended CombinationWhy it works
Essay / Writing 🎯 Personalized + 🔄 Process Over Product Personal grounding blocks AI substitution; process documentation proves the thinking was the student's own
Research Project 🔗 Scaffolded + 📍 Local Context Staged submissions prevent last-minute AI completion; local sources can't be fabricated by AI
Case Analysis 🎯 Personalized + 🎤 Beyond AI Requires industry-specific personal knowledge plus a live defense — both block AI substitution at once
Discussion Post 🧑 Personal Experience + 🎯 Personalized Both require authentic personal content; together they make generic AI responses obvious and insufficient
Lab / Technical Report 🔄 Process Over Product + 📍 Local Context Actual lab data and annotated methodology can't be credibly fabricated; process portfolio validates authentic work
Capstone / Seminar All three: 🎯 + 🔄 + 🎤 High-stakes work merits maximum resistance: personal grounding, visible process, and live defense together create a robust authentic-work requirement
💡 A note on workload Stronger resistance often means more to grade. The Multilayer / Scaffolded and Process Over Product strategies in particular add submission components. Before selecting them, consider whether you have the bandwidth to review and respond to multiple stages — especially in large enrollment courses. Two well-chosen strategies applied consistently will do more for assignment integrity than five strategies applied inconsistently.
