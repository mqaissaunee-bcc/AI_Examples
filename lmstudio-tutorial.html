Common Mistakes & Fixes
Things that trip up most beginners.
❌Model runs very slowly: GPU layers may not be enabled. In LM Studio → model settings, set n_gpu_layers to -1. Also verify your GPU is recognized under Settings → Hardware.
❌"Out of memory" crash: Your model is too large for your RAM. Switch to a smaller quantization (e.g., from Q8 to Q4_K_M) or pick a smaller parameter count (e.g., 7B instead of 13B).
❌Responses cut off mid-sentence: Increase Max Tokens in the generation settings. Default may be too low for long responses.
⚠️Model repetition / loops: Increase Repeat Penalty to 1.15 or higher. This discourages the model from getting stuck in a loop.
⚠️Slow download speeds: Models are large (2–8 GB). Use a wired connection if possible. Downloads resume automatically if interrupted.
💡Apple Silicon tip: Make sure you select the MLX or Metal-optimized version of the model when available. These are dramatically faster on M1/M2/M3/M4 chips.
✅Best practice: Start with a Q4_K_M quantized model. It offers the best balance of quality, speed, and RAM usage for most computers.

Understanding Quantization
What do Q4, Q8, GGUF, MLX mean?

AI models are huge files. Quantization compresses them by reducing the precision of each number, making them smaller and faster at a small quality cost. Think of it like image compression — JPEG vs RAW.

FormatSizeQualityBest For
Q2_KTiny⭐⭐Very low RAM systems (4–6 GB). Noticeable quality drop.
Q4_K_MSmall⭐⭐⭐⭐✅ Sweet spot. Best balance of quality and size. Start here.
Q5_K_MMedium⭐⭐⭐⭐½If you have 16 GB+ RAM and want slightly better quality.
Q8_0Large⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐Near-perfect quality. Needs 16–32 GB RAM. For power users.
