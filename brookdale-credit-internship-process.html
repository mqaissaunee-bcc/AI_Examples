Timeline · 8 steps in detail

Each step below corresponds to a node in the flowchart. Start the process at least one semester ahead.

1

Confirm eligibility

Before applying Self-service

Review the Step 0 eligibility self-check above. All criteria must be met before you apply or accept an offer.

  • Matriculated in a degree-seeking program
  • At least 6 Brookdale credits earned (one full semester)
  • 2.0+ GPA — some programs require higher
  • Required major coursework completed
2

Find an internship

Prior semester · ongoing Two paths

Path A — Use Handshake (recommended). Activate your account at brookdalecc.joinhandshake.com and browse postings from employers who partner with Brookdale.

Path B — You already have an offer. Proceed to Step 3 to contact the Career Center.

3

Make an appointment with the Career Center

24–48 hr reply Required

Email career@brookdalecc.edu before applying or accepting any internship. The Career Center will respond within 24–48 hours (excluding weekends) to schedule an intake appointment.

This appointment is required even if you already have an offer in hand — it's how the Career Center reviews fit and outlines next steps.

4

Submit the intake form

If you have an offer Documentation

If you arrived with an internship offer, the Career Center will direct you to the intake form during your appointment. Complete it and provide:

  • The internship offer letter (uploaded)
  • The name of the faculty member who will oversee the internship
5

Obtain the three required approvals

Allow 1–2 weeks Approval gate

Before registration, three approvals must be in place:

  • Faculty instructor in your major — reviews academic fit and will oversee the experience
  • Dean of the academic area — authorizes the internship from the division
  • Career Center representative — confirms site suitability and Career Center approval

Plan for this to take 1–2 weeks. Initiate well before the semester's registration deadline.

6

Employer submits required documentation

Employer task On company letterhead

The sponsoring employer must submit documentation on company letterhead to the Career Center including:

  • Student's assignment and internship duties
  • Rate of pay (if paid)
  • Number of hours per week
  • Name and contact information of the designated supervisor
  • Confirmation that the work is related to your field of study
  • Assurance that you will complete at least 45 hours per credit
7

Register for the internship course

Required By registration deadline

Once all approvals are in place, register for the appropriate internship / cooperative education course through Web Advisor / Self Service.

The specific course number varies by academic department — examples include COOP, INTR, or a department-specific prefix. Registration follows normal Brookdale procedures and deadlines.

8

Complete the internship and final evaluation

End of semester Graded

During the internship you must:

  • Complete all learning objectives defined at the start
  • Be available for on-site visits by your faculty representative and/or a Career Center representative

The employer completes evaluation forms provided by Brookdale — these directly affect your grade. You'll have a final evaluation meeting with a Career Center representative upon completion.

Important notes

Things to know

  • All internships must be on file with the Career Center — no exceptions.
  • Internship sites are reviewed by the Career Center for appropriateness.
  • Unpaid internships must comply with the U.S. Department of Labor's requirements for unpaid interns.
  • Any changes in internship status must be reported to the Career Center immediately.
  • The employer's evaluation forms directly impact your grade and program standing.
  • Start at least one semester ahead — approvals and documentation take time.
